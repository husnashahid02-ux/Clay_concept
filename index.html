01 — The problem I lived

Outbound is broken by design — I know because I did it

Before I moved into product, I worked in sales. And the single biggest frustration wasn't the rejection — it was how much time went into work that should have been automated. Manual research. Generic messaging. Fragmented tools that didn't talk to each other. Spreadsheets duct-taped to sequencers.

The high performers weren't working harder. They were pulling signals from places others didn't think to look — hiring pages, funding news, LinkedIn activity, job description language. That's not talent. That's a workflow gap that product should close.

"I didn't know what I needed then. Looking at Clay now, I do — this is exactly it."

I've since moved into product — working across logistics, SaaS, edtech, fintech, and now AI automation. But the sales pain never left me. If anything, being on the product side made it clearer how solvable it actually is.

02 — The Clay angle

What Clay uniquely enables

Clay collapses the research → enrich → score → message workflow into a single programmable surface. That's not incremental improvement — that's a category shift. The fact that it's built for operators who want to customize logic (not just toggle a setting) is exactly why it resonates with people who felt the problem most acutely.

What I find most compelling isn't the feature set — it's the philosophy. Clay bets that outbound quality is a data architecture problem, not a volume problem. That's the right bet. And it's one I now understand from both sides of the table.

03 — What I'd build

A behavioral signal scoring workflow

Here's a concrete workflow I'd architect using Clay — built around the signals I wish I had access to as a sales rep, structured the way I'd design it as a PM.

lead-scoring-workflow.clay
1
Ingest a company list
Pull from CRM export, LinkedIn search, or any CSV. Clay becomes the enrichment layer on top — not a replacement for your existing lists.
input
2
Enrich with behavioral signals
Funding recency, headcount growth, open roles in Sales/Ops, tech stack changes, LinkedIn activity from key decision-makers. Waterfall enrichment keeps cost low.
enrich
3
Score by intent tier
Formula-based scoring using weighted signals: Tier 1 (hiring + funded + tech-fit), Tier 2 (one strong signal), Tier 3 (cold). Reps only touch T1 and T2.
score
4
Generate context-aware messages
Use Clay's AI column to draft personalized first lines based on the top signal per account. Not a template — a starting point that's actually specific.
ai column
5
Route to sequence
Push enriched, scored, and messaged leads directly to your sequencer (Outreach, Apollo, Salesloft). The rep reviews and sends — no more research tab-switching.
output
